Midway

The battle of Midway was often known as the turning point of the war in the Pacific, but was it really? Could the Japanese really keep up to the level of production that the US could? They were printing ships like no tomorrow. Think about this: Iowas were being printed in half a years time, escort carriers came in 6 weeks and they had more Essex class fleet carriers see combat than the Japanese had Armoured Fleet Carriers, Fleet Carriers, Light Carriers, Escort Carriers and Amphibious Assault Ships total. Then consider there were over 100 US Escort Carriers, many other fleet and light carriers on top of having the transport capacity to keep the fleet going...
